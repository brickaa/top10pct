In 1990, 52 percent of Texans age 17 to 19 were white, 13 percent were black and 32 percent were Hispanic.

But you probably couldn't tell that looking at the freshmen undergradute class at the University of Texas in Austin — because in 1990, 83.5 percent of freshmen students were white, 4 percent were black and 12.5 percent were Hispanic.

Fifteen years later, Hispanics are now the majority in the 17 to 19 year old age group at 48 percent, while the proportion of blacks is still 13 percent and the white population has decreased to 33 percent.

And you can see a slight shift in the student body at UT — as 73 percent of the 2015 freshman class is white, while 22 percent is Hispanic.

But the proportion of black students has hardly changed — increasing from 4 to 5 percent over 15 years.
